The Evolution of Cinema: The Success Story Behind Box-Office Hits (1915‚Äì2015) explores the critical factors that influenced the success of films during this transformative period. From the golden age of Hollywood to the global dominance of franchises, this study traces the trajectory of cinema as both an art form and a global industry. Through a data-driven analysis of box-office trends spanning 100 years, we aim to uncover the patterns that define cinematic success. What genres have stood the test of time? By answering these questions, this project offers a deeper understanding of the intersection between creativity, commerce, and culture in the world of cinema.

Datasets

Our project leverages two comprehensive datasets to analyze the factors influencing box-office success for films released between 1915 and 2015. These datasets provide complementary information about movie characteristics, ratings, revenue, and metadata, enabling us to conduct an in-depth analysis of audience preferences, industry trends, and their impact on box-office performance. Below, we introduce the two datasets we will use:

CMU Movie Summary Corpus

Created by researchers at Carnegie Mellon University, this dataset contains metadata for 42,306 movies, including:

Metadata Provided:

  • Box-office revenue
  • Genre(s)
  • Release date
  • Runtime
  • Language(s)
  • Country of origin

Actor and Character Information:

  • Character names
  • Actor details (name, gender, age, height, ethnicity)

Supplementary Data:

  • Processed summaries using Stanford CoreNLP
  • Named entity recognition (NER)
  • Coreference resolution

Source: CMU Movie Summary Corpus

IMDb Datasets

Comprehensive movie information from IMDb, including:

Key Files:

  • title.basics.tsv.gz Movie metadata, titles, genres, release years, runtime
  • title.ratings.tsv.gz IMDb ratings and vote counts

Analysis Capabilities:

  • Audience ratings correlation with success
  • Localization strategies impact
  • Genre popularity trends
  • Movie reach and revenue patterns

Source: IMDb Data Files
